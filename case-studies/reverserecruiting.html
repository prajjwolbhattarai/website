Reverse Recruiting Automation

Increasing EU Job Seekers’ Success

Context

In the European job market, many skilled job seekers face frequent rejections. The main issues were non-optimized resumes, generic cover letters, and manual applications that did not pass ATS systems.

I built a reverse recruiting automation system to help candidates by scraping jobs, ranking them by match, and automatically generating tailored resumes and cover letters — significantly improving their chances of landing interviews.

Challenge

  • High rejection rates due to ATS filters.
  • Manual application process was slow and repetitive.
  • Resumes and cover letters lacked customization for each job.

The goal was to automate the entire application pipeline while keeping outputs ATS-friendly and personalized enough to stand out.

Goals

  • Automate job discovery and application workflows.
  • Improve interview callback rates for candidates.
  • Ensure ATS compliance with tailored, keyword-rich resumes.

My Role

  • Architected the automation pipeline end-to-end.
  • Built resume & cover letter generators using GPT.
  • Integrated job scraping + ranking algorithms.

Approach

Job Discovery & Ranking

  • Built scrapers to collect jobs from major boards.
  • Implemented ranking logic based on keyword and profile matching.

Resume & Cover Letter Generation

  • Used GPT prompts to generate ATS-optimized resumes.
  • Ensured job-specific customization by pulling keywords from descriptions.

Application Automation

  • Connected pipeline to apply directly via portals (where APIs allowed).
  • Used Google Sheets + Make to manage flows and logs.

Tools & Platforms

GPT (resume/cover letter generation)Google SheetsMake (Integromat)Job scraping APIs

Key Learnings

  • Automation increased throughput, but human review improved success rates further.
  • ATS compliance (keywords, formatting) was critical for interview invitations.
  • Ranking saved time — candidates focused only on the best-fit jobs.

Impact

This became a productized recruiting solution with scalable potential. It not only improved outcomes for individual job seekers but also demonstrated how automation can disrupt recruitment.
