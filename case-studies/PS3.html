Pocket Studio

Driving Adoption of the Billing Software Module

Context

After repositioning Pocket Studio as an all-in-one restaurant management SaaS, the company launched a Billing Software Module aimed at streamlining invoices, payments, and financial reporting for restaurants. This feature had strong potential for upsell into the existing base and acquisition of new clients — but adoption was slow.

My role was to design and execute a performance marketing strategy specifically for the Billing Software Module that would generate qualified demo requests and drive usage within restaurants.

The Challenge

Existing Tool Silos

Restaurants were using separate invoicing and accounting tools (Xero, QuickBooks), creating friction for adopting a new integrated solution.

Perceived Value

The billing module was seen as “nice-to-have” instead of mission critical, hindering its perceived necessity for daily operations.

Ineffective Campaigns

Paid campaigns had high impressions but low click-to-demo conversion, indicating a disconnect between awareness and intent.

Weak Tracking

Tracking of module adoption was weak compared to core product metrics, making it difficult to measure true impact and optimize.

Strategic Goals

  • Increase module demo requests by 2X in 6 months.
  • Reduce CPL (Cost Per Lead) for module-specific campaigns.
  • Build awareness among both new prospects and existing Pocket Studio clients.
  • Establish a tracking framework for feature adoption.

My Role & Responsibilities

I owned the end-to-end go-to-market campaign for the module, encompassing:

  • Designed separate funnel and landing pages specifically for the billing software.
  • Ran multi-channel campaigns with ROI-focused angles to maximize efficiency.
  • Implemented adoption dashboards to measure usage after conversion and track post-signup engagement.

Strategic Approach

1. ICP Segmentation

  • Existing Clients: Upsell via targeted email campaigns and strategic in-platform prompts.
  • New Clients: Targeted campaigns focusing on restaurants that actively searched for “restaurant invoicing” or “POS with billing.”

2. Funnel Strategy

  • TOF (Top of Funnel): LinkedIn Ads featuring thought-leadership content (e.g., “Why restaurants lose 15% efficiency using multiple tools”).
  • MOF (Middle of Funnel): Google Search Ads capturing high-intent terms like “restaurant billing software” combined with retargeting ads.
  • BOF (Bottom of Funnel): Dedicated landing pages offering compelling ROI calculators (e.g., “Save €5K/year on billing tools”).

3. Advanced Tracking

  • Implemented GA4 events for precise module sign-up tracking.
  • Integrated adoption tracking inside Pocket Studio via HubSpot + Tableau dashboards for comprehensive insights.
  • Built UTM flows specifically tailored to the billing module for granular campaign attribution.

4. Messaging & Creative

  • Highlighted the profound integration benefits (“One tool, one login, one invoice”).
  • Focused on tangible cost savings + time efficiency rather than merely listing features.

Key Learnings

  • Dedicated Funnels are Crucial: Feature modules need their own funnels; when bundled with the core product, they were often overlooked.
  • ROI Calculators Drive Conversion: Providing clear financial benefit through ROI calculators significantly improved demo conversion rates.
  • Post-Signup Tracking is Vital: Tracking usage after signup provided invaluable insights into potential churn risks and feature stickiness.

Overall Impact

Pocket Studio’s billing module transformed from an underutilized feature to a strategic growth driver. This initiative not only boosted new acquisitions and upsells but also significantly improved customer stickiness and lifetime value across the entire SaaS platform.
